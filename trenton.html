CHAPTER REPORT: TRENTON M.A.K.E.S

"Serve the People; Fight the Power"
⚠ URGENT: 4 COMRADES DETAINED BY STATE SECURITY

1. The Housing Question & Urban Decay

Fig 1. Productive forces lay idle while the people suffer.

The objective conditions in Trenton reveal the deep contradictions of late-stage capitalism. Vast blocks of abandoned industrial warehouses and residential structures stand fenced and empty—monuments to private property rights—while the streets overflow with displaced proletarians.

The Trenton Rescue Mission, currently the only state-sanctioned refuge, is overcrowded and reflects the failure of the bourgeois social safety net. We reject the charity model. We demand the immediate seizure of empty housing for the homeless. The people have a right to shelter, derived from their labor, not the benevolence of the ruling class.

Analysis: The city of Trenton is a "ghost shell" not because of a lack of resources, but because of mismanagement and exploitation. Our chapter organizes regular inspections of these zones to document the neglect and prepare for mass action.

2. Pan-Africanism & The Sahel Alliance

Fig 2. The Alliance of Sahel States (AES) breaks the chains of ECOWAS.

The Trenton M.A.K.E.S chapter stands in full solidarity with the Alliance of Sahel States (AES). The recent formation of this confederation—Mali, Burkina Faso, and Niger—marks a decisive break from neocolonial structures like ECOWAS and the Françafrique monetary system.

Imperialist media labels these nations as "unstable," yet we see the rapid reclamation of sovereignty. Under the leadership of Captain Ibrahim Traoré, Burkina Faso has demonstrated that true independence leads to rapid development.

INFRASTRUCTURE REPORT: BURKINA FASO

COLONIAL ERA: 3,000 km of roads built in 80 years.
AES ERA (TRAORÉ): 5,000 km of NEW ROADS per YEAR.

Socialism builds. Imperialism extracts.

This massive infrastructure investment is not for the extraction of wealth for Paris, but for the connection of the Burkinabé people. We salute this military alliance as a necessary shield against Western aggression and a model for the Global South.

3. Combatting Local Imperialist Collaboration

While our communities crumble, the state of New Jersey actively collaborates with the Zionist apartheid regime through the NJ-Israel Commission. This is a clear example of local imperialism. Trade is prioritized over human rights, and our tax dollars are used to fund oppression abroad.

True internationalism requires us to stand against all forms of imperialist aggression. The struggle in Trenton is directly linked to the struggle in Palestine. We oppose the revisionist policies that normalize these relations.

Following the confrontation in Livingston, four members of our cell were illegally detained by the repressive state apparatus. They are currently being held as political prisoners. We are launching a full-scale legal and political defense campaign.

We call on all progressive organizations and individuals to flood the phones of the prosecutor's office and demand their immediate release. An injury to one is an injury to all.
